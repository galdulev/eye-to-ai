# Revision Priority Matrix — "Scaling Good" by Gal Dulev

## Methodology

Scored using the book's own **Debt Inventory** framework (Chapter 7):

| Dimension | Scale | Definition |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| **Severity (S)** | 1–5 | Damage to the book's credibility, utility, or reception |
| **Cost of Delay (C)** | 1–5 | Consequence of NOT fixing before publication |
| **Effort to Resolve (E)** | 1–5 | Work required (1 = paragraph, 5 = structural rewrite) |
| **Priority Score** | — | (S × C) ÷ E |

### Tier Thresholds

| Tier | Score Range | Label |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **1** | ≥ 8.0 | MUST FIX BEFORE PUBLICATION |
| **2** | 4.0 – 7.9 | SHOULD FIX IF TIME PERMITS |
| **3** | 2.0 – 3.9 | NICE TO HAVE |
| **4** | < 2.0 | ACKNOWLEDGE IN AUTHOR'S NOTE |

## NARRATIVE: THE TOP 10 PRIORITIES

### 1. Fix the Amazon Factual Error (Score: 20.0)

The single easiest and highest-impact fix in the entire book. Amazon's first full-year profit was 2003 — roughly nine years after founding, not twenty. This is a Google-verifiable factual error in Chapter 8, and it will be the first thing any hostile reviewer latches onto to discredit the entire evidence base. **Fix: Change one sentence.** Time required: 5 minutes.

### 2. Cite Dan Pallotta on the Overhead Myth (Score: 20.0)

Pallotta's TED talk on the overhead myth has over 5 million views. His book *Uncharitable* (2008) made the same core argument as Chapter 8. Not citing him doesn't make the overlap invisible — it makes it look like plagiarism or ignorance. Either perception is catastrophic. **Fix: Add one citation and a positioning sentence** ("Building on Pallotta's foundational critique..."). Time required: 15 minutes.

### 3. Add Composite/Fictionalization Disclosure for Anonymous Anecdotes (Score: 16.0)

Nine out of ten social-sector anecdotes are anonymous with no disclosure that they may be composites. All it takes is one journalist, academic, or hostile reviewer to try verifying the $3M youth center, the housing program with 22%→61% results, or the Nov 2010 software incident — and find nothing. A simple author's note ("Names and identifying details have been changed; some examples are composites drawn from multiple organizations") transforms the book's relationship with its evidence. **Fix: One paragraph in the author's note, plus brief inline disclosure where needed.** Time required: 30 minutes.

### 9. Address the Absence of Racial Equity and DEI (Score: 8.3)

### 10. Acknowledge the Book's Own Irony — Zero Framework Validation (Score: 8.3)

The most intellectually devastating finding across all eleven review steps: the book proposes a comprehensive framework for evidence-based practice and provides zero evidence that the framework itself works. This isn't fatal if handled honestly. The fix is a forthright preface paragraph: "This book proposes a framework drawn from [X] years of observation and [Y] conversations. It has not been formally validated. That is both a limitation I want to name honestly and an invitation." This turns vulnerability into credibility. **Fix: One paragraph in the preface + recurring language of "proposed framework" rather than "proven framework."**
